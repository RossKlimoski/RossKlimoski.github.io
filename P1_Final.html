Timeline of Time

Earliest humans used various environmental markers to keep track of seasons and crop harvests

3000 BC - Stonehenge built in Wiltshire England

1500 BC - Sundials first used by Egyptians

400 BC - Greeks use outflow of water from a vessel to make a water clock

1000 - Europe begins using burning candles to measure time

1400 - Mechanical clocks built in Europe, using a mainspring and balance wheel

1657 - Christiaan Huygens invents first pendulum clock that is capable of far greater accuracy than any preceding timekeeper

1839 - Instant transmission of time signals is made possible by the invention of the telegraph

1884 - 25 countries accept Greenwich England as the prime meridian

1949 - National Institute of Standards and Technology(formerly the National Bureau of Standards) builds the first atomic clock

1967 - A second is formally defined as 9,192,631,770 vibrations of the cesium atom

Present Day - Hundreds of millions of watches are sold each year and time guides the actions of society more than ever
